Books

01

Cacioppo, J. T., & Patrick, W. (2008).

Loneliness: Human Nature and the Need for Social Connection. W. W. Norton & Company. This book explores the biological and social mechanisms behind loneliness, and how it affects mental health and overall well-being.

02

Hawkley, L. C., & Cacioppo, J. T. (2010).

Loneliness and Health: Potential Mechanisms. Psychosomatic Medicine, 72(3), 199-211. This research discusses the connection between loneliness and various health outcomes, including its psychological and physiological impacts.

Online Resources

01

The Campaign to End Loneliness

Website: www.campaigntoendloneliness.org A resource hub offering various articles, research, and guides on tackling loneliness and social isolation, with a focus on older adults.

02

National Institute on Aging (NIA) - Loneliness and Social Isolation

Website: www.nia.nih.gov/news/loneliness-and-social-isolation A government resource that provides insights into the effects of loneliness, particularly in older populations, and offers resources for addressing the issue.

03

American Psychological Association (APA) - Loneliness

Website: www.apa.org/topics/loneliness The APA’s dedicated page on loneliness offers summaries of current research, tips for coping, and societal impacts.

04

Mental Health Foundation - Lonelines

Website: www.mentalhealth.org.uk/publications/loneliness A nonprofit organization providing in-depth reports on loneliness, its relationship to mental health, and interventions to reduce it.

Reports and Studies

01

AARP Foundation - Loneliness and Social Isolation: The Impact on Older Adults

Report: www.aarp.org/research/topics/community/info-2020/loneliness-and-social-isolation.html This report from AARP examines the specific impact of loneliness on older adults and offers recommendations for reducing isolation.

02

World Health Organization (WHO) - Ageing and Health: Loneliness

Report: www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/ageing-and-health WHO’s report highlights the global health implications of loneliness and isolation, with a focus on elderly populations.
